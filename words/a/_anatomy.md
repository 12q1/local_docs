# Anatomy
---
#noun
- **The [[a/_art|art]] of studying the [[d/_different|different]] parts of [[a/_any|any]] organized [[b/_body|body]], to [[d/_discover|discover]] their situation, structure, and [[e/_economy|economy]].**
- **The science that [[d/_deals|deals]] with the form and structure of organic [[b/_bodies|bodies]]; [[a/_anatomical|anatomical]] structure or organization.**
	- _Example: Animal anatomy is also called zootomy; vegetable anatomy, phytotomy; and human anatomy, anthropotomy._
- **A treatise or [[b/_book|book]] on [[a/_anatomy|anatomy]].**
- **(by extension) The [[a/_act|act]] of [[d/_dividing|dividing]] [[a/_anything|anything]], [[c/_corporeal|corporeal]] or intellectual, for the purpose of examining its parts.**
	- _Example: Burton's famous treatise, "The Anatomy of Melancholy"_
- **The form of an individual.**
	- _Example: I went to the Venice beach body-building competition and noticed the competitor from Athens, and let me tell you, that's what I call classic Greek anatomy._
- **The human [[b/_body|body]], especially in reference to the private parts.**
- **A skeleton, or [[d/_dead|dead]] [[b/_body|body]].**
- **The physical or functional organization of an organism, or part of it.**
