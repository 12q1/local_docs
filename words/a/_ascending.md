# Ascending
---
#verb
- **To move upward, to fly, to soar.**
	- _Example: He ascended to heaven upon a cloud._
- **To slope in an upward direction.**
- **To go up.**
	- _Example: You ascend the stairs and take a right._
- **To succeed.**
	- _Example: She ascended the throne when her mother abdicated._
- **To rise; to [[b/_become|become]] higher, more noble, etc.**
- **To trace, search or go [[b/_backwards|backwards]] temporally (e.g., through records, genealogies, routes, etc.).**
	- _Example: Our inquiries ascend to the remotest antiquity._
- **To [[b/_become|become]] higher in pitch.**

#noun
- **An [[a/_ascent|ascent]].**
	- _Example: continual ascendings and descendings_

#adjective
- **(of a sequence) Ordered such that each element is greater than or equal to the previous element.**
	- _Example: Please arrange these numbers in an ascending order._
- **That [[c/_causes|causes]] a sequence to follow an [[a/_ascending|ascending]] order.**
	- _Example: We used an ascending sort._
