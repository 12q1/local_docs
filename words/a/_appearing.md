# Appearing
---
#verb
- **To [[c/_come|come]] or be in sight; to be in view; to [[b/_become|become]] visible.**
- **To [[c/_come|come]] [[b/_before|before]] the public.**
	- _Example: A great writer appeared at that time._
- **To stand in presence of some [[a/_authority|authority]], tribunal, or superior person, to [[a/_answer|answer]] a [[c/_charge|charge]], plead a [[c/_cause|cause]], [[e/_etc|etc]].; to present oneself as a party or [[a/_advocate|advocate]] [[b/_before|before]] a [[c/_court|court]], or as a person to be tried.**
- **To [[b/_become|become]] visible to the [[a/_apprehension|apprehension]] of the mind; to be known as a subject of observation or [[c/_comprehension|comprehension]], or as a thing proved; to be obvious or manifest.**
- **To seem; to have a [[c/_certain|certain]] semblance; to look.**
	- _Example: He appeared quite happy with the result._
- **To [[b/_bring|bring]] into view.**
