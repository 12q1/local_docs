# Ambulatory
---
#noun
- **The round walkway [[e/_encircling|encircling]] the [[a/_altar|altar]] in many [[c/_cathedrals|cathedrals]].**
- **[[a/_any|Any]] part of a [[b/_building|building]] intended for walking in; a [[c/_corridor|corridor]].**
---
#adjective
- **Of, relating to, or [[a/_adapted|adapted]] to walking**
	- _Example: ambulatory exercise_
- **[[a/_able|Able]] to walk [[a/_about|about]] and not [[b/_bedridden|bedridden]].**
	- _Example: an ambulatory patient_
- **Performed on or involving an [[a/_ambulatory|ambulatory]] patient or an outpatient.**
	- _Example: ambulatory medical care_
- **[[a/_accustomed|Accustomed]] to move from place to place; not stationary; movable.**
	- _Example: an ambulatory court, which exercises its jurisdiction in different places_
- **Not yet legally fixed or settled; [[a/_alterable|alterable]].**
	- _Example: The dispositions of a will are ambulatory until the death of the testator._
