# Appropriate
---
#verb
- **To make suitable; to suit.**
- **To take to oneself; to [[c/_claim|claim]] or use, especially as by an exclusive right.**
	- _Example: Let no man appropriate the use of a common benefit._
- **To set [[a/_apart|apart]] for, or [[a/_assign|assign]] to, a particular person or use, especially in exclusion of [[a/_all|all]] others; with to or for.**
	- _Example: A spot of ground is appropriated for a garden._
- **To [[a/_annex|annex]] (for example a [[b/_benefice|benefice]], to a spiritual [[c/_corporation|corporation]], as its property).**
---
### Synonyms
- [[allocate]], [[earmark]], [[help oneself]], [[impropriate]]
#adjective
- **Suitable or fit; proper.**
	- _Example: The headmaster wondered what an appropriate measure would be to make the pupil behave better._
- **Suitable to the social situation or to social respect or social discreetness; socially [[c/_correct|correct]]; socially discreet; well-mannered; proper.**
	- _Example: I don't think it was appropriate for the cashier to tell me out loud in front of all those people at the check-out that my hair-piece looked like it was falling out of place._
- **Set [[a/_apart|apart]] for a particular use or person; reserved.**
