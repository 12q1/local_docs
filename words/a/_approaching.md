# Approaching
---
#verb
- **To [[c/_come|come]] or go near, in place or time; to draw nigh; to [[a/_advance|advance]] nearer.**
- **To draw near, in a figurative sense; to make [[a/_advances|advances]]; to [[a/_approximate|approximate]].**
	- _Example: as he approaches to the character of the ablest statesman._
- **To [[c/_come|come]] near to in place, time, [[c/_character|character]] or value; to draw nearer to.**
	- _Example: "Would counsel please approach the bench?" asked the judge._
- **To make an [[a/_attempt|attempt]] at (solving a problem or making a policy).**
- **To speak to, as to make a request or [[a/_ask|ask]] a question.**
- **To take [[a/_approaches|approaches]] to.**
- **To [[b/_bring|bring]] near; to [[c/_cause|cause]] to draw near.**
