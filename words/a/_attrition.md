# Attrition
---
#noun
- **Wearing or grinding down by friction.**
- **The gradual reduction in a tangible or intangible resource due to [[c/_causes|causes]] that are passive and do not involve productive use of the resource.**
- **(human resources) A gradual, natural reduction in membership or personnel, as through retirement, resignation, or [[d/_death|death]].**
- **The loss of participants during an experiment.**
- **Imperfect [[c/_contrition|contrition]] or remorse.**
- **The wearing of teeth due to their grinding.**
- **The loss of a first or second language or a portion of that language.**
---

### Antonyms

#verb
- **To grind or wear down through friction.**
	- _Example: attritioned teeth; attritioned rock_
- **To reduce the number of (jobs or workers) by not hiring new employees to fill positions that [[b/_become|become]] vacant (often with out).**
	- _Example: As a result of the new policy, people were being attritioned out._
- **To undergo a reduction in number.**
	- _Example: The cohort of one hundred students had attritioned to sixty by the end of secondary school._
