# Broaching
---
#verb
- **To make a hole in, [[e/_especially|especially]] a [[c/_cask|cask]] of liquor, and put in a tap in order to [[d/_draw|draw]] the liquid.**
- **To open, to make an opening into; to pierce.**
	- _Example: French knights at Agincourt were unable to broach the English line._
- **To [[b/_begin|begin]] [[d/_discussion|discussion]] [[a/_about|about]] (something).**
	- _Example: I broached the subject of contraceptives carefully when the teenager mentioned his promiscuity._
---
#verb
- **To be turned sideways to oncoming waves, [[e/_especially|especially]] large or [[b/_breaking|breaking]] waves.**
	- _Example: The small boat broached and nearly sank, because of the large waves._
- **To [[c/_cause|cause]] to turn sideways to oncoming waves, [[e/_especially|especially]] large or [[b/_breaking|breaking]] waves (usually followed by to; [[a/_also|also]] figurative).**
	- _Example: Each time we came around into the wind, the sea broached our bow._
