# Betray
---
#verb
- **To [[d/_deliver|deliver]] into the hands of an enemy by treachery or fraud, in violation of trust; to give up treacherously or faithlessly.**
	- _Example: an officer betrayed the city_
- **To prove faithless or treacherous to, as to a trust or one who trusts; to be false to; to [[d/_deceive|deceive]].**
	- _Example: My eyes have been betraying me since I turned sixty._
- **To violate the [[c/_confidence|confidence]] of, by disclosing a secret, or that which one is [[b/_bound|bound]] in honor not to make known.**
- **To disclose or indicate, for example something which prudence would [[c/_conceal|conceal]]; to reveal unintentionally.**
	- _Example: Though he had lived in England for many years, a faint accent betrayed his Swedish origin._
- **To mislead; to expose to inconvenience not foreseen; to lead into error or sin.**
- **To lead [[a/_astray|astray]]; to seduce (as under promise of marriage) and then [[a/_abandon|abandon]].**
