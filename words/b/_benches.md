# Benches
---
#noun
- **A long seat with or without a [[b/_back|back]], found for example in parks and schools.**
	- _Example: They sat on a park bench and tossed bread crumbs to the ducks and pigeons._
- **The people who [[d/_decide|decide]] on the verdict; the judiciary.**
	- _Example: They are awaiting a decision on the motion from the bench._
- **The place where the judges sit.**
	- _Example: She sat on the bench for 30 years before she retired._
- **The dignity of holding an official seat.**
	- _Example: the bench of bishops_
- **The place where players (substitutes) and [[c/_coaches|coaches]] sit when not playing.**
	- _Example: He spent the first three games on the bench, watching._
- **The number of players on a team [[a/_able|able]] to participate, expressed in terms of length.**
	- _Example: Injuries have shortened the bench._
- **A place where [[a/_assembly|assembly]] or hand work is performed; a workbench.**
	- _Example: She placed the workpiece on the bench, inspected it closely, and opened the cover._
- **A horizontal padded surface, usually [[a/_adjustable|adjustable]] in height and inclination and often with [[a/_attached|attached]] weight rack, used for proper posture during exercise.**
- **A [[b/_bracket|bracket]] used to mount land surveying equipment onto a stone or a wall.**
	- _Example: After removing the bench, we can use the mark left on the wall as a reference point._
- **A flat ledge in the slope of an earthwork, work of masonry, or similar.**
- **A thin strip of relatively flat land [[b/_bounded|bounded]] by steeper slopes [[a/_above|above]] and [[b/_below|below]].**
- **A kitchen surface on which to prepare food, a [[c/_counter|counter]].**
- **A [[b/_bathroom|bathroom]] surface which holds the washbasin, a vanity.**
- **A [[c/_collection|collection]] or group of dogs exhibited to the public, traditionally on [[b/_benches|benches]] or raised platforms.**
