# Backfire
---
#noun
- **A small, [[c/_controlled|controlled]] fire set in the path of a larger uncontrolled fire, in order to limit the spread of the large fire by removing its fuel.**
- **An explosion produced either by a running internal [[c/_combustion|combustion]] engine that occurs in the [[a/_air|air]] intake or exhaust system rather than inside the [[c/_combustion|combustion]] [[c/_chamber|chamber]] or unburned fuel or hydrocarbons ignited somewhere in the exhaust system.**
---
#noun
- **A premature explosion in the [[c/_cylinder|cylinder]] of a gas or oil engine during the exhaust or the [[c/_compression|compression]] stroke, tending to drive the piston in the wrong direction.**
- **An explosion in the exhaust passages of an internal [[c/_combustion|combustion]] engine.**
---
### Synonyms
- [[knock]], [[ping]]
#verb
- **(of a gun, [[c/_cannon|cannon]], [[b/_bunsen|Bunsen]] [[b/_burner|burner]], etc.) To fire in the opposite direction, for example due to an obstruction in the [[b/_barrel|barrel]].**
- **(of an engine) To experience a premature ignition of fuel or an ignition of exhaust gases, making a popping sound.**
- **To fail in a manner that [[b/_brings|brings]] down further misfortune.**
	- _Example: His attempt to make money by importing luxury cars backfired horribly when fuel prices tripled._
