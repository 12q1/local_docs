# Broad

#noun
- **A shallow lake, one of a number of [[b/_bodies|bodies]] of water in eastern Norfolk and Suffolk.**
- **A lathe tool for turning down the insides and [[b/_bottoms|bottoms]] of [[c/_cylinders|cylinders]].**

#adjective
- **Wide in extent or scope.**
	- _Example: the broad expanse of ocean_
- **Extended, in the sense of diffused; open; [[c/_clear|clear]]; full.**
- **Having a large measure of [[a/_any|any]] thing or quality; unlimited; unrestrained.**
- **[[c/_comprehensive|Comprehensive]]; liberal; enlarged.**
- **Plain; evident.**
	- _Example: a broad hint_
- **(writing) Unsubtle; obvious.**
- **Free; unrestrained; unconfined.**
- **Gross; [[c/_coarse|coarse]]; indelicate.**
	- _Example: a broad compliment; a broad joke; broad humour_
- **(of an [[a/_accent|accent]]) Strongly regional.**
- **(Gaelic languages) Velarized, i.e. not palatalized.**
