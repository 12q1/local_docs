# Boggle
---
#noun
- **A scruple or objection.**
- **A [[b/_bungle|bungle]]; a [[b/_botched|botched]] situation.**
---
#verb
- **Either literally or figuratively to stop or hesitate as if suddenly seeing a [[b/_bogle|bogle]].**
	- _Example: The dogs went on, but the horse boggled at the sudden appearance of the strange beast._
- **To be [[b/_bewildered|bewildered]], dumbfounded, or [[c/_confused|confused]].**
	- _Example: He boggled at the surprising news._
- **To [[c/_confuse|confuse]] or mystify; overwhelm.**
	- _Example: The oddities of quantum mechanics can boggle the minds of students and experienced physicists alike._
- **To embarrass with difficulties; to palter or equivocate; to [[b/_bungle|bungle]] or [[b/_botch|botch]].**
- **To dissemble; to play fast and loose (with someone or something).**
