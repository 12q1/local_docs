# Blackmail
---
#noun
- **The extortion of money by threats of public [[a/_accusation|accusation]], exposure, or [[c/_censure|censure]].**
- **A form of protection money (or [[c/_corn|corn]], [[c/_cattle|cattle]], [[e/_etc|etc]].) [[a/_anciently|anciently]] paid, in the north of [[e/_england|England]] and south of Scotland, to the [[a/_allies|allies]] of robbers in order to be spared from pillage.**
- **[[b/_black|Black]] rent, or rent paid in [[c/_corn|corn]], meat, or the lowest [[c/_coin|coin]], as opposed to white rent, which was paid in silver.**
- **[[c/_compromising|Compromising]] material that [[c/_can|can]] be used to extort someone, [[d/_dirt|dirt]].**
---
#verb
- **To extort money or favors from (a person) by exciting fears of injury other than [[b/_bodily|bodily]] harm, such as injury to reputation, [[d/_distress|distress]] of mind, false [[a/_accusation|accusation]], [[e/_etc|etc]].**
	- _Example: He blackmailed a businesswoman by threatening to expose an alleged fraud._
- **(Kenya) To speak ill of someone; to [[d/_defame|defame]] someone.**
