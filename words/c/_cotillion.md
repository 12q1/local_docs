#noun
- **A [[b/_bold|bold]] [[d/_dance|dance]] performed in groups of [[e/_eight|eight]] where women lift their skirts to [[d/_display|display]] their [[a/_ankles|ankles]].**
	- _Example: 1797 Mrs. Hughes now joined them, and asked Miss Tilney if she was ready to go. "I hope I shall have the pleasure of seeing you again soon," said Catherine. "Shall you be at the cotillion ball tomorrow?" Jane Austin Northanger Abbey (written 1797 first published 1818) Chapter 10._
- **The music regulating the [[c/_cotillion|cotillion]].**
	- _Example: 1848 I kept a parlor open for the reception of visitors, many came here to practise with me, and many more to listen to us—several young men put themselves under my tuition, and although I had never been taught myself, they progressed finely in their studies and I soon brought out, not only the best field music, but also for dinners, balls, cotilion and tea parties, weddings, &c.
