# Concluded
---
#verb
- **To end; to [[c/_come|come]] to an end.**
	- _Example: The story concluded with a moral._
- **To [[b/_bring|bring]] to an end; to [[c/_close|close]]; to finish.**
- **To [[b/_bring|bring]] [[a/_about|about]] as a result; to effect; to make.**
	- _Example: to conclude a bargain_
- **To [[c/_come|come]] to a [[c/_conclusion|conclusion]], to a final [[d/_decision|decision]].**
	- _Example: From the evidence, I conclude that this man was murdered._
- **To make a final [[d/_determination|determination]] or judgment [[c/_concerning|concerning]]; to judge; to [[d/_decide|decide]].**
- **To shut off; to restrain; to limit; to estop; to [[b/_bar|bar]]; generally in the passive.**
	- _Example: A judgment concludes the introduction of further evidence._
- **To shut up; to enclose.**
- **To include; to [[c/_comprehend|comprehend]]; to shut up together; to embrace.**
- **To [[d/_deduce|deduce]], to infer (develop a [[c/_causal|causal]] relation)**
