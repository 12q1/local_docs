#verb
- **To press [[f/_forward|forward]]; to [[a/_advance|advance]] by pushing.**
	- _Example: The man crowded into the packed room._
- **To press together or [[c/_collect|collect]] in [[n/_numbers|numbers]]**
	- _Example: They crowded through the archway and into the park._
- **To press or [[d/_drive|drive]] together, [[e/_especially|especially]] [[i/_into|into]] a small space; to [[c/_cram|cram]].**
	- _Example: He tried to crowd too many cows into the cow-pen._
- **To [[f/_fill|fill]] by pressing or thronging together**
- **([[o/_often|often]] used with "[[o/_out|out]] of" or "[[o/_off|off]]") To push, to press, to shove.**
	- _Example: They tried to crowd her off the sidewalk._
- **To [[a/_approach|approach]] [[a/_another|another]] ship too [[c/_closely|closely]] when it [[h/_has|has]] right of way.**
- **(of a square-rigged ship) To [[c/_carry|carry]] [[e/_excessive|excessive]] sail in the [[h/_hope|hope]] of [[m/_moving|moving]] [[f/_faster|faster]].**
- **To press by solicitation; to urge; to [[d/_dun|dun]]; [[h/_hence|hence]], to treat [[d/_discourteously|discourteously]] or unreasonably.**
