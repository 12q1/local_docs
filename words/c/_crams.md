# Crams
---
#noun
- **The [[a/_act|act]] of [[c/_cramming|cramming]] (forcing or stuffing something).**
- **Information hastily memorized.**
	- _Example: a cram from an examination_
- **A warp having more than two threads passing through each [[d/_dent|dent]] or split of the reed.**
- **([[b/_british|British]] slang) A lie; a falsehood.**
- **A mathematical [[b/_board|board]] game in which players take turns placing dominoes horizontally or vertically until no more [[c/_can|can]] be placed, the loser [[b/_being|being]] the player who [[c/_cannot|cannot]] [[c/_continue|continue]].**
- **A small friendship [[b/_book|book]] with limited space for people to enter their information.**
---
#verb
- **To press, force, or drive, particularly in filling, or in thrusting one thing into [[a/_another|another]]; to stuff; to fill to superfluity.**
	- _Example: to cram fruit into a basket; to cram a room with people_
- **To fill with food to satiety; to stuff.**
	- _Example: The boy crammed himself with cake_
- **To put hastily through an extensive [[c/_course|course]] of memorizing or study, as in preparation for an examination.**
	- _Example: A pupil is crammed by his tutor._
- **To study hard; to swot.**
- **To eat greedily, and to satiety; to stuff oneself.**
- **([[b/_british|British]] slang) To lie; to intentionally not tell the truth.**
- **([[b/_british|British]] slang) To make (a person) [[b/_believe|believe]] false or exaggerated tales.**
