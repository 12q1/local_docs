# Craft
---
#noun
- **Strength; power; might; force .**
- **Intellectual power; skill; [[a/_art|art]].**
- **(obsolete in the general sense) A work or product of [[a/_art|art]] .**
- **A [[d/_device|device]], a means; a magical [[d/_device|device]], spell or [[e/_enchantment|enchantment]] .**
- **Learning of the schools, scholarship; a [[b/_branch|branch]] of learning or knowledge, a science, [[e/_especially|especially]] one of the ‘seven liberal [[a/_arts|arts]]’ of the medieval universities .**
- **Skill, skilfulness, [[a/_art|art]], [[e/_especially|especially]] the skill needed for a particular profession .**
	- _Example: The craft of writing plays._
- **(plural [[c/_crafts|crafts]]) A [[b/_branch|branch]] of skilled work or trade, [[e/_especially|especially]] one requiring manual [[d/_dexterity|dexterity]] or [[a/_artistic|artistic]] skill, but sometimes [[a/_applied|applied]] [[e/_equally|equally]] to [[a/_any|any]] [[b/_business|business]], [[c/_calling|calling]] or profession; the skilled practice of a practical occupation .**
	- _Example: He learned his craft as an apprentice._
- **A trade or profession as [[e/_embodied|embodied]] in its practitioners [[c/_collectively|collectively]]; the members of a trade or handicraft as a [[b/_body|body]]; an [[a/_association|association]] of these; a trade's union, guild, or ‘[[c/_company|company]]’ .**
	- _Example: She represented the craft of brewers._
- **(plural [[c/_craft|craft]]) A vehicle [[d/_designed|designed]] for navigation in or on water or [[a/_air|air]] or through outer space .**
- **Implements used in [[c/_catching|catching]] fish, such as net, line, or hook. Modern use primarily in whaling, as in harpoons, hand-lances, [[e/_etc|etc]]. .**
