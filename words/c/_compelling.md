# Compelling
---
#verb
- **To drive together, round up**
- **To overpower; to subdue.**
- **To force, [[c/_constrain|constrain]] or [[c/_coerce|coerce]].**
	- _Example: Logic compels the wise, while fools feel compelled by emotions._
- **To exact, extort, (make) produce by force.**
- **To force to yield; to overpower; to subjugate.**
- **To gather or unite in a [[c/_crowd|crowd]] or [[c/_company|company]].**
- **To [[c/_call|call]] forth; to summon.**
---
#noun
- **An [[a/_act|act]] of [[c/_compulsion|compulsion]]; an obliging somebody to do something.**
---
#adjective
- **Strongly or irresistibly evoking interest or [[a/_attention|attention]].**
	- _Example: There are compelling reasons why a manager should have previous experience._
- **Forceful.**
	- _Example: The politician had compelling ambition._
