# Causes
---
#noun
- **(often with of, typically of [[a/_adverse|adverse]] results) The source of, or reason for, an event or [[a/_action|action]]; that which produces or [[e/_effects|effects]] a result.**
	- _Example: They identified a burst pipe as the cause of the flooding._
- **(especially with for and a [[b/_bare|bare]] noun) Sufficient reason for a state, as of [[e/_emotion|emotion]].**
	- _Example: The end of the war was a cause for celebration._
- **A goal, [[a/_aim|aim]] or principle, especially one which transcends purely selfish [[e/_ends|ends]].**
- **Sake; interest; [[a/_advantage|advantage]].**
- **[[a/_any|Any]] subject of [[d/_discussion|discussion]] or [[d/_debate|debate]]; a matter; an [[a/_affair|affair]].**
- **A suit or [[a/_action|action]] in [[c/_court|court]]; [[a/_any|any]] legal process by which a party [[e/_endeavors|endeavors]] to obtain his [[c/_claim|claim]], or what he regards as his right; [[c/_case|case]]; ground of [[a/_action|action]].**

#verb
- **To set off an event or [[a/_action|action]].**
	- _Example: The lightning caused thunder._
- **(ditransitive) To [[a/_actively|actively]] produce as a result, by means of force or [[a/_authority|authority]].**
	- _Example: His dogged determination caused the fundraising to be successful._
- **To [[a/_assign|assign]] or show [[c/_cause|cause]]; to give a reason; to make excuse.**
