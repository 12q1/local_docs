# Colophons
---
#noun
- **In manuscripts (typically [[b/_before|before]] the invention of printing), the note, usually at the end, left by the scribe who [[c/_copied|copied]] it, giving information on his exemplar, where and when the [[c/_copy|copy]] was made, and sometimes, his own name.**
- **A printer's or publisher's identifying inscription or logo [[a/_appearing|appearing]] at the front or end of a [[b/_book|book]], or the same [[a/_appearing|appearing]] on the spine or dust-jacket. It generally [[c/_contains|contains]] factual information [[a/_about|about]] the [[b/_book|book]], especially [[a/_about|about]] its production, and includes [[d/_details|details]] [[a/_about|about]] typographic style, the fonts used, the paper used, and perhaps the [[b/_binding|binding]] method of the [[b/_book|book]]. [[a/_also|Also]] used in a similar fashion for newspapers, magazines, and [[a/_academic|academic]] journals.**
- **A page on a website identifying the [[d/_details|details]] of its [[c/_creation|creation]], such as the [[a/_author|author]]'s name and the technologies used.**
- **A finishing stroke or [[c/_crowning|crowning]] touch.**
