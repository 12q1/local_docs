# Harbored
---
#verb
- **To provide a [[h/_harbor|harbor]] or safe place for.**
	- _Example: The docks, which once harbored tall ships, now harbor only petty thieves._
- **To take refuge or shelter in a protected [[e/_expanse|expanse]] of water.**
	- _Example: The fleet harbored in the south._
- **To [[d/_drive|drive]] (a [[h/_hunted|hunted]] stag) to [[c/_covert|covert]].**
- **To [[h/_hold|hold]] or persistently [[e/_entertain|entertain]] in [[o/_one|one]]'s thoughts or [[m/_mind|mind]].**
	- _Example: She harbors a conviction that her husband has a secret, criminal past._
