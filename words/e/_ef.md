#conjunction
- **Supposing that, [[a/_assuming|assuming]] that, in the [[c/_circumstances|circumstances]] that; used to introduce a [[c/_condition|condition]] or [[c/_choice|choice]].**
	- _Example: If it rains, I shall get wet._
- **In the event that a statement is true (a programming statement that [[a/_acts|acts]] in a similar manner).**
	- _Example: If A, then B, else C._
- **Supposing that; used with past or past perfect subjunctive indicating that the [[c/_condition|condition]] is [[c/_closed|closed]].**
	- _Example: I would be unhappy if you had not talked with me yesterday._
- **Supposing that; given that; supposing it is the [[c/_case|case]] that.**
	- _Example: If that's true, we had better get moving!_
- **[[a/_although|Although]]; used to introduce a [[c/_concession|concession]].**
	- _Example: He was a great friend, if a little stingy at the bar._
- **(sometimes proscribed) Whether; used to introduce a noun [[c/_clause|clause]], an indirect question, that functions as the [[d/_direct|direct]] object of [[c/_certain|certain]] verbs.**
	- _Example: I don't know if I want to go or not._
- **(usually hyperbolic) Even if; even in the [[c/_circumstances|circumstances]] that.**
- **Introducing a relevance [[c/_conditional|conditional]].**
	- _Example: I have leftover cake if you want some._
