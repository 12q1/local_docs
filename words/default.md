# Default
---
#noun
- **The [[condition]] of failing to meet an obligation.**
	- _Example: He failed to make payments on time and is now in default._
- **The original software programming settings as set by the factory**
- **A loss incurred by failing to [[compete]].**
	- _Example: The team's three losses include one default._
- **A selection made in the [[absence]] of an [[alternative]].**
	- _Example: The man became the leader of the group as a default._
- **A value used when none has [[been]] given; a tentative value or standard that is presumed.**
	- _Example: If you don't specify a number of items, the default is 1._
- **The failure of a [[defendant]] to [[appear]] and [[answer]] a summons and [[complaint]].**
- **A failing or failure; omission of that which ought to be done; neglect to do what duty or law requires.**
	- _Example: This evil has happened through the governor's default._
- **Lack; [[absence]].**
- **Fault; offence; wrong [[act]].**
---
#verb
- **To fail to meet an obligation.**
	- _Example: If you do not make your payments, you will default on your loan._
- **To lose a [[competition]] by failing to [[compete]].**
	- _Example: If you refuse to wear a proper uniform, you will not be allowed to compete and will default this match._
- **To [[assume]] a value when none was given; to presume a tentative value or standard.**
	- _Example: If you don't specify a number of items, it defaults to 1._
- **To fail to [[appear]] and [[answer]] a summons and [[complaint]].**
