# Dictations
---
#noun
- **[[d/_dictating|Dictating]], the process of speaking for someone [[e/_else|else]] to write [[d/_down|down]] the words**
	- _Example: Since I learned shorthand, I can take dictation at eighty words a minute._
- **An [[a/_activity|activity]] in school where the teacher reads a [[p/_passage|passage]] [[a/_aloud|aloud]] and the students write it [[d/_down|down]]**
	- _Example: 1908: Lucy Maud Montgomery, Anne of Green Gables - We had reading and geography and Canadian history and dictation today._
- **The [[a/_act|act]] of [[o/_ordering|ordering]] or [[c/_commanding|commanding]]**
	- _Example: 1852: Lysander Spooner, An Essay on the Trial by Jury - ...jurors in England have formerly understood it to be their right and duty to judge only according to their consciences, and not to submit to any dictation from the court, either as to law or fact._
- **[[o/_orders|Orders]] [[g/_given|given]] in an [[o/_overbearing|overbearing]] [[m/_manner|manner]]**
	- _Example: His habit, even with friends, was that of dictation._
