# Daylight
---
#noun
- **The light from the Sun, as opposed to that from [[a/_any|any]] other source.**
- **A light source that simulates [[d/_daylight|daylight]].**
- **(photometry) The intensity distribution of light over the visible spectrum generated by the Sun under various [[c/_conditions|conditions]] or by other light sources intended to simulate natural [[d/_daylight|daylight]].**
- **The period of time [[b/_between|between]] sunrise and sunset.**
	- _Example: We should get home while it's still daylight._
- **[[d/_daybreak|Daybreak]].**
	- _Example: We had only two hours to work before daylight._
- **Exposure to public scrutiny.**
	- _Example: Budgeting a spy organization can't very well be done in daylight._
- **A [[c/_clear|clear]], open space.**
	- _Example: All small running backs instinctively run to daylight._
- **(machinery) The space [[b/_between|between]] platens on a press or similar machinery.**
	- _Example: The minimum and maximum daylights on an injection molding machine determines the sizes of the items it can make._
- **Emotional or psychological distance [[b/_between|between]] people, or disagreement.**
	- _Example: We completely agree. There's no daylight between us on the issue._

#verb
- **To expose to [[d/_daylight|daylight]]**
- **To provide sources of natural illumination such as skylights or windows.**
- **To [[a/_allow|allow]] light in, as by opening drapes.**
- **(landscaping) To run a drainage pipe to an opening from which its [[c/_contents|contents]] [[c/_can|can]] drain [[a/_away|away]] naturally.**
- **To gain exposure to the open.**
	- _Example: The seam of coal daylighted at a cliff by the river._
