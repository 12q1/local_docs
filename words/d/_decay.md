# Decay
---
#noun
- **The process or result of [[b/_being|being]] gradually [[d/_decomposed|decomposed]].**
- **A [[d/_deterioration|deterioration]] of [[c/_condition|condition]]; loss of status or fortune.**

#verb
- **To [[d/_deteriorate|deteriorate]], to get worse, to lose strength or health, to [[d/_decline|decline]] in quality.**
	- _Example: The pair loved to take pictures in the decaying hospital on forty-third street._
- **(of organic material) To rot, to go [[b/_bad|bad]].**
	- _Example: The cat's body decayed rapidly._
- **(of an unstable [[a/_atom|atom]]) To [[c/_change|change]] by undergoing fission, by [[e/_emitting|emitting]] radiation, or by [[c/_capturing|capturing]] or losing one or more [[e/_electrons|electrons]].**
- **(of a quantum system) To undergo optical [[d/_decay|decay]], that is, to relax to a less excited state, usually by [[e/_emitting|emitting]] a photon or phonon.**
- **Loss of [[a/_airspeed|airspeed]] [[d/_due|due]] to [[d/_drag|drag]].**
- **To [[c/_cause|cause]] to rot or [[d/_deteriorate|deteriorate]].**
	- _Example: The extreme humidity decayed the wooden sculptures in the museum's collection in a matter of years._
