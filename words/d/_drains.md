# Drains
---
#noun
- **A [[c/_conduit|conduit]] [[a/_allowing|allowing]] liquid to flow out of an otherwise [[c/_contained|contained]] volume; a plughole (UK)**
	- _Example: The drain in the kitchen sink is clogged._
- **An [[a/_access|access]] point or [[c/_conduit|conduit]] for rainwater that [[d/_drains|drains]] [[d/_directly|directly]] [[d/_downstream|downstream]] in a ([[d/_drainage|drainage]]) [[b/_basin|basin]] without going through sewers or water treatment in order to prevent or [[b/_belay|belay]] floods.**
- **Something [[c/_consuming|consuming]] resources and providing nothing in return.**
	- _Example: That rental property is a drain on our finances._
- **An [[a/_act|act]] of urination.**
- **One terminal of a field [[e/_effect|effect]] transistor (FET).**
- **An outhole.**
- **A [[d/_drink|drink]].**

#verb
- **To lose liquid.**
	- _Example: The clogged sink drained slowly._
- **To flow gradually.**
	- _Example: The water of low ground drains off._
- **To [[c/_cause|cause]] liquid to flow out of.**
	- _Example: Please drain the sink. It's full of dirty water._
- **To [[c/_convert|convert]] a perennially wet place into a [[d/_dry|dry]] one.**
	- _Example: They had to drain the swampy land before the parking lot could be built._
- **To [[d/_deplete|deplete]] of [[e/_energy|energy]] or resources.**
	- _Example: The stress of this job is really draining me._
- **To [[d/_draw|draw]] off by [[d/_degrees|degrees]]; to [[c/_cause|cause]] to flow gradually out or off; hence, to exhaust.**
- **To filter.**
- **To fall off the [[b/_bottom|bottom]] of the playfield.**
