# Divorce
---
#noun
- **The legal [[d/_dissolution|dissolution]] of a marriage.**
	- _Example: Richard obtained a divorce from his wife some years ago, but hasn't returned to the dating scene._
- **A separation of [[c/_connected|connected]] things.**
	- _Example: The Civil War split between Virginia and West Virginia was a divorce based along cultural and economic as well as geographic lines._
- **That which separates.**
---
### Synonyms
- [[divorcement]], [[partition]], [[separation]], [[severance]]
### Antonyms
- [[marriage]]
#verb
- **To legally [[d/_dissolve|dissolve]] a marriage [[b/_between|between]] two people.**
	- _Example: A ship captain can marry couples, but cannot divorce them._
- **To [[e/_end|end]] one's own marriage to (a person) in this way.**
	- _Example: Lucy divorced Steve when she discovered that he had been unfaithful._
- **To obtain a legal [[d/_divorce|divorce]].**
	- _Example: Edna and Simon divorced last year; he got the house, and she retained the business._
- **To separate something that was [[c/_connected|connected]].**
	- _Example: The radical group voted to divorce itself from the main faction and start an independent movement._
