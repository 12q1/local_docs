# Diaeresis
---
#noun
- **A [[d/_diacritic|diacritic]] ( ¨ ) placed over a vowel letter ([[e/_especially|especially]] the second of two [[c/_consecutive|consecutive]] ones) indicating that it is sounded separately, usually forming a [[d/_distinct|distinct]] syllable, as in the [[e/_english|English]] words naïve, Noël and Brontë, the French haïr and the [[d/_dutch|Dutch]] ruïne.**
- **[[d/_distraction|Distraction]]; the separation of a vowel, often a [[d/_diphthong|diphthong]], into two [[d/_distinct|distinct]] syllables.**
- **A natural [[b/_break|break]] in rhythm when a word [[e/_ends|ends]] at the [[e/_end|end]] of a metrical foot, in a line of verse.**
- **Hiatus; the occurrence of separate vowel sounds in [[a/_adjacent|adjacent]] syllables without an intervening [[c/_consonant|consonant]].**
