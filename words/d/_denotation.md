# Denotation
---
#noun
- **The [[a/_act|act]] of [[d/_denoting|denoting]], or something (such as a symbol) that [[d/_denotes|denotes]]**
- **The primary, surface, literal, or explicit meaning of a signifier such as a word, phrase, or symbol; that which a word [[d/_denotes|denotes]], as [[c/_contrasted|contrasted]] with its [[c/_connotation|connotation]]; the [[a/_aggregate|aggregate]] or set of objects of which a word may be predicated.**
	- _Example: The denotations of the two expressions "the morning star" and "the evening star" are the same (i.e. both expressions denote the planet Venus), but their connotations are different._
- **The intension and extension of a word**
- **Something signified or referred to; a particular meaning of a symbol**
- **[[a/_any|Any]] mathematical object which [[d/_describes|describes]] the meanings of expressions from the languages, formalized in the theory of [[d/_denotational|denotational]] semantics**
- **(media studies) A first level of [[a/_analysis|analysis]]: what the [[a/_audience|audience]] [[c/_can|can]] visually see on a page. [[d/_denotation|Denotation]] often refers to something literal, and [[a/_avoids|avoids]] [[b/_being|being]] a metaphor.**
