# Desk
---
#noun
- **A table, frame, or [[c/_case|case]], in past [[c/_centuries|centuries]] usually with a sloping top but now usually with a flat top, for the use of writers and readers. It often has a [[d/_drawer|drawer]] or repository underneath.**
- **A reading table or lectern to support the [[b/_book|book]] from which the liturgical service is read, [[d/_differing|differing]] from the pulpit from which the sermon is preached; [[a/_also|also]] (especially in the United States), a pulpit. Hence, used symbolically for the [[c/_clerical|clerical]] profession.**
- **A [[d/_department|department]] of a newspaper tasked with [[c/_covering|covering]] a particular geographical region or [[a/_aspect|aspect]] of the news.**
	- _Example: city desk_
---
#verb
- **To shut up, as in a [[d/_desk|desk]]; to treasure.**
- **To [[e/_equip|equip]] with a [[d/_desk|desk]] or [[d/_desks|desks]].**
