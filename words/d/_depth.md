# Depth
---
#noun
- **The vertical distance below a surface; the degree to which something is deep**
	- _Example: Measure the depth of the water in this part of the bay._
- **The distance between the front and the back, as the depth of a drawer or closet**
- **The intensity, complexity, strength, seriousness or importance of an emotion, situation, etc.**
	- _Example: The depth of her misery was apparent to everyone._
- **Lowness**
	- _Example: the depth of a sound_
- **(colors) the total palette of available colors**
- **The property of appearing three-dimensional**
	- _Example: The depth of field in this picture is amazing._
- **(usually in the plural) the deepest part (usually of a body of water)**
	- _Example: The burning ship finally sunk into the depths._
- **(usually in the plural) a very remote part.**
	- _Example: In the depths of the night,_
- **The most severe part**
	- _Example: in the depth of the crisis_
- **The number of simple elements which an abstract conception or notion includes; the comprehension or content**
- **A pair of toothed wheels which work together**
- **The perpendicular distance from the chord to the farthest point of an arched surface**
- **The lower of the two ranks of a value in an ordered set of values**
