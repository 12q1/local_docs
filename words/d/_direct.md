# Direct
---
#verb
- **To manage, [[c/_control|control]], steer.**
	- _Example: to direct the affairs of a nation or the movements of an army_
- **To [[a/_aim|aim]] (something) at (something [[e/_else|else]]).**
	- _Example: He directed his question to the room in general._
- **To point out or show to (somebody) the right [[c/_course|course]] or way; to guide, as by pointing out the way.**
	- _Example: He directed me to the left-hand road._
- **To point out to with [[a/_authority|authority]]; to instruct as a superior; to order.**
	- _Example: She directed them to leave immediately._
- **To put a [[d/_direction|direction]] or [[a/_address|address]] upon; to mark with the name and residence of the person to whom [[a/_anything|anything]] is sent.**
	- _Example: to direct a letter_
---
#adjective
- **Proceeding without [[d/_deviation|deviation]] or interruption.**
- **Straight; not [[c/_crooked|crooked]], oblique, or [[c/_circuitous|circuitous]]; leading by the short or shortest way to a point or [[e/_end|end]].**
	- _Example: the most direct route between two buildings_
- **Straightforward; sincere.**
- **Immediate; express; plain; unambiguous.**
- **In the line of [[d/_descent|descent]]; not [[c/_collateral|collateral]].**
	- _Example: a descendant in the direct line_
- **In the [[d/_direction|direction]] of the general planetary motion, or from west to [[e/_east|east]]; in the order of the signs; not retrograde; said of the motion of a [[c/_celestial|celestial]] [[b/_body|body]].**
- **Pertaining to, or [[e/_effected|effected]] immediately by, [[a/_action|action]] of the people through their votes instead of through one or more representatives or [[d/_delegates|delegates]].**
	- _Example: direct nomination; direct legislation_
- **(travel) having a single flight number.**
