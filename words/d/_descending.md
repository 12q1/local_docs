# Descending
---
#verb
- **To pass from a higher to a lower place; to move downwards; to [[c/_come|come]] or go down in [[a/_any|any]] way, for example by falling, flowing, walking, [[c/_climbing|climbing]] etc.**
	- _Example: The rain descended, and the floods came._
- **To enter mentally; to retire.**
- **(with on or upon) To make an [[a/_attack|attack]], or incursion, as if from a vantage ground; to [[c/_come|come]] suddenly and with violence.**
	- _Example: And on the suitors let thy wrath descend._
- **To [[c/_come|come]] down to a lower, less fortunate, humbler, less virtuous, or worse, state or rank; to lower or [[a/_abase|abase]] oneself**
	- _Example: he descended from his high estate_
- **To pass from the more general or important to the specific or less important matters to be [[c/_considered|considered]].**
- **To [[c/_come|come]] down, as from a source, original, or stock**
- **To be [[d/_derived|derived]] (from)**
- **To proceed by generation or by transmission; to happen by inheritance.**
	- _Example: A crown descends to the heir._
- **To move toward the south, or to the southward.**
- **To fall in pitch; to pass from a higher to a lower tone.**
- **To go down upon or [[a/_along|along]]; to pass from a higher to a lower part of**
	- _Example: they descended the river in boats; to descend a ladder_

#adjective
- **(of a sequence) Ordered such that each element is less than or equal to the previous element.**
	- _Example: Please arrange these numbers in a descending order._
- **That [[c/_causes|causes]] a sequence to follow a [[d/_descending|descending]] order.**
	- _Example: We used a descending sort._
