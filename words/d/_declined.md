# Declined
---
#verb
- **To move downwards, to fall, to drop.**
	- _Example: The dollar has declined rapidly since 2001._
- **To [[b/_become|become]] weaker or worse.**
	- _Example: My health declined in winter._
- **To [[b/_bend|bend]] downward; to [[b/_bring|bring]] down; to [[d/_depress|depress]]; to [[c/_cause|cause]] to [[b/_bend|bend]], or fall.**
- **To [[c/_cause|cause]] to [[d/_decrease|decrease]] or diminish.**
- **To turn or [[b/_bend|bend]] [[a/_aside|aside]]; to deviate; to stray; to withdraw.**
	- _Example: a line that declines from straightness_
- **To refuse, forbear.**
	- _Example: On reflection I think I will decline your generous offer._
- **(grammar, usually of substantives, [[a/_adjectives|adjectives]] and pronouns) To inflect for [[c/_case|case]], number and sometimes gender.**
- **(by extension) To run through from first to last; to repeat like a schoolboy [[d/_declining|declining]] a noun.**
- **To reject a penalty [[a/_against|against]] the opposing team, usually [[b/_because|because]] the result of [[a/_accepting|accepting]] it would [[b/_benefit|benefit]] the non-penalized team less than the preceding play.**
	- _Example: The team chose to decline the fifteen-yard penalty because their receiver had caught the ball for a thirty-yard gain._
