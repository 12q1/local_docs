# Declensions
---
#noun
- **A falling off, [[d/_decay|decay]] or [[d/_descent|descent]].**
- **(grammar) The [[a/_act|act]] of [[d/_declining|declining]] a word; the [[a/_act|act]] of listing the inflections of a noun, pronoun or [[a/_adjective|adjective]] in order.**
- **(grammar) A way of [[c/_categorizing|categorizing]] nouns, pronouns, or [[a/_adjectives|adjectives]] [[a/_according|according]] to the inflections they receive.**
	- _Example: In Latin, 'amicus' belongs to the second declension. Most second-declension nouns end in '-i' in the genitive singular and '-um' in the accusative singular._
