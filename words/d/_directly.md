---
#adverb
- **In a [[d/_direct|direct]] manner; in a straight line or [[c/_course|course]].**
	- _Example: He drove directly to the office, and didn't stop off at the petrol station._
- **In a straightforward way; without [[a/_anything|anything]] intervening; not by secondary, but by [[d/_direct|direct]] means.**
	- _Example: I'm sick of asking you to fire him, I'll just do it directly._
- **Plainly, without [[c/_circumlocution|circumlocution]] or [[a/_ambiguity|ambiguity]]; [[a/_absolutely|absolutely]]; in express terms.**
	- _Example: I'm going to tell Natalie directly that I love her._
- **Exactly; just.**
	- _Example: It's directly across the street._
- **Straightforwardly; honestly.**
	- _Example: He told me directly that he'd cheated on me, and how sorry he was for it._
- **Immediately.**
- **Soon; next; when it [[b/_becomes|becomes]] [[c/_convenient|convenient]].**
	- _Example: We'll go to the store directly, but first I need to finish sweeping._
