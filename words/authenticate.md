# Authenticate
---
#adjective
- **Of the same origin as claimed; genuine.**
	- _Example: The experts confirmed it was an authentic signature._
- **Conforming to reality and therefore worthy of trust, reliance, or belief.**
	- _Example: The report was completely authentic._
- **(of a Gregorian mode) Having the final as the lowest note of the mode.**
- **Authoritative**
---
### Synonyms
- [[credible]], [[reliable]], [[trustworthy]], [[unfaked]], [[bona fide]], [[bonafide]], [[genuine]], [[real]], [[unfaked]]
### Antonyms
- [[fake]], [[ingenuine]], [[phony]]
#verb
- **To render [[authentic]]; to give [[authority]] to, by the proof, [[attestation]], or formalities required by law, or sufficient to entitle to credit.**
- **To prove [[authentic]]; to determine as real and true.**
	- _Example: He didn't manage to authenticate the portrait: it's probably a fake._
